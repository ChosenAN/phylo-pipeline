Full workflow guide

The complete Pelegrini Lab pipeline from UMAP selection to Phylo.io comparison, with automated steps highlighted.

1
Go to UMAP of Life v2. The map contains Archaea + Bacteria + Eukaryota organisms (23,656 PFAMs, correlation metric). Use Select mode to draw a lasso around your clade of interest.
2
After selection, click Export CSV. The CSV contains species info in the format:
[Name] [ID: taxID] | taxonomy path | x, y
Paste this into Step 1 of this tool to parse it.
3
Automated Use Step 1 to parse species and Step 2 to extract taxIDs one per line.
4
Copy the taxID list from Step 2. Go to NCBI CommonTree, paste the IDs, select Phylip tree format, and click Save as: to download the .phy file.
5
Automated Paste the .phy content into Step 2 to convert to clean Newick format and send it to the taxonomy tree. This is your taxonomy tree.
6
Back on the UMAP site, with your selection still active, click Make Phylogenetic Tree (based on domain count), then Download Newick. This is your domain tree.
7
Automated Paste both Newick strings into Step 3 to compute Robinson-Foulds distance, leaf overlap, and discordance statistics.
8
For visual side-by-side comparison, go to Phylo.io, upload both Newick files, and enable color-coded topological similarity. RF distance from Step 3 tells you the magnitude of discordance between domain-based and taxonomic groupings.
💡 What RF distance means here: A low RF score means domain co-occurrence patterns (Pfam profiles) recover the same phylogenetic relationships as formal taxonomy — the protein domain landscape mirrors evolutionary history. A high RF score reveals clades where horizontal gene transfer, convergent evolution, or taxonomic revision may explain the mismatch.
